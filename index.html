1. THE STILLING

Many decades ago, during a global solar eclipse, the sun stopped moving. It did not vanish. It did not explode. It simply froze in place. From that moment on there was no sunrise or sunset. Never again a real day or night.

The world was left in a permanent, dim twilight. The sun still glows faintly in the sky, but it no longer warms the land or feeds plant life the way it once did.

This moment became the dividing line in history: "Before the Stilling" and "After the Stilling"

Timekeeping became a cultural problem. Different groups invented their own measurements of time based on:

  • Crystal glow cycles
  • Bioluminescent plant pulses
  • Creature migration patterns

Over the years the planet their name changed to "Evershade"

2. CURRENT WORLD STATE

Light & Atmosphere

  • Constant dim blue-grey lighting everywhere
  • No clear shadows, only soft gradients
  • Air often feels heavy and wet
  • Fog settles easily and stays for long periods
  • Sound travels farther than before

The world feels:

  • Quiet
  • Slow
  • Suspended
  • Half-asleep

3. FLORA SYSTEM

General Rules

  • Almost all surviving plants are bioluminescent, carnivorous or parasitic, and chemical-based feeders
  • Most plants are active, not passive
  • The world follows a basic truth: The more beautiful the plant, the more dangerous it is

Known Flora

  • Sweetbreath Traps: Plants that release hypnotic fragrance. Victims feel calm and euphoric. Roots pull unconscious bodies underground.
  • Graveblossoms: Flowers that grow from dead wood. They feed on decay and emit soft glows in silver, pale blue, or violet.
  • Wellsap Vines: Water-producing plants that collect moisture. Some safe, others contain poisons.
  • Glassleaf Shrubs: Transparent leaves that cut skin. They refract light to lure prey.
  • Pulse Moss: Carpet-like moss that expands and contracts, shifting ground and swallowing objects.
  • Memory Reeds: Tall plants that whisper when touched, possibly storing echoes of past events.
  • Corpse Lanterns: Fungus that grows on buried skeletons and glows stronger with more bone.

4. FAUNA SYSTEM

General Rules

  • Most creatures rely on echolocation, scent trails, or produce their own light
  • Eyes are often replaced by heat sensing pits, sound receptors, or vibrational whiskers

Known Fauna

  • Bulbskulls: Small creatures with oversized storage heads for water and nutrients.
  • Echo-Wings: Bat-like fliers with sonar navigation and armored wings.
  • Beacon Tailed Wanderers: Snake or lizard-like creatures that glow along their bodies.
  • Drift Hunters: Jellyfish-like floaters that pulse light and sting prey.
  • Shardback Beasts: Thick-skinned animals with glowing crystal growths.
  • Hollow Howlers: Blind quadrupeds that sense movement and disorient with calls.
  • Rootleeches: Worm-like creatures in plant roots that drink sap or blood.

5. INTELLIGENT FOLK & ENTITIES

Known Groups

  • The Deepstone Cities: Underground crystal-powered civilizations. Use glowing crystals for light and energy. Culture based on order and conservation.
  • The Bloodless Forest Kin (Tree Beings): Branch-and-roots bodies. Ritual-based culture with forest markings.
  • The Watchers: Shadow creatures that feed on blood and warmth. Trigger feeling of being watched.
  • The Glass Maskers: Humans wearing transparent masks. Cautious culture dependent on rare plants.
  • The Lantern Keepers: Nomads bonded with glowing creatures. Use living light as partners.
  • The Sunbound: Cult devoted to restoring the sun. Worship brightness and perform rituals with reflective artifacts.

6. LANDSCAPES & REGIONS

Known Biomes

  • Glowforests: Forests of luminous trees and fungi.
  • Root Seas: Underground regions with massive root systems.
  • Crystal Hollows: Caverns with glowing mineral formations.
  • The Soft Cities: Ruins covered in moss and plants that move buildings.
  • The Breathing Marsh: Wetlands where ground rises and falls.
  • The Pale Ridges: Mountains that reflect sky-glow, appearing ghostly.
  • The Hanging Gravefields: Forests with dead trees suspended by vines.

7. TECHNOLOGY & SURVIVAL

Common tools:

Methods of navigation:

8. CULTURAL BELIEFS

Common myths about the sun:

  • The sun is asleep
  • The sun is dead
  • The sun is trapped
  • The sun is watching
